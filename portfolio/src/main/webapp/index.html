ABOUT ME

Hello there! I am a rising junior studying Computer Science and Business at Oregon State University. Outside of the classroom, I enjoy being part of TALI, a group of undergraduate students interested in teaching K-12 students about STEM and Computer Science. Learning about Computer Science early on allows students to develop strong problem-solving skills which can be applied to their education and beyond. In the future, I hope to continue engaging students in technology at a young age. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, exploring Oregon, and planning new adventures.

ABROAD

After high school, I received a scholarship to participate in a year-long program in Germany where I had the opportunity to live with a host-family, participate in hands-on internships, and learn German. My experiences in Germany increased my interest in travel and language learning, which motivated me to study abroad during college. Last summer, I studied abroad at Yonsei University in Seoul, South Korea. Thanks to the Benjamin A. Gilman International and Critical Language Scholarships, I was able to take courses related to my major and study Korean. My most memorable experiences included participating in Taekwondo after school, making life-long friendships, and learning to navigate the public transportation system in a new country. Thanks to these experiences, I have gained a lifetime of memories and a unique global perspective.
